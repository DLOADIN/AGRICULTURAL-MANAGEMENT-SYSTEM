COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

Domestic Competitors: Within Cameroon, coffee producers and cooperatives are key competitors. Some notable competitors include Kawa Cooperative, Moungo cooperative, and COOPAC Cooperative. These cooperatives often produce specialty coffee beans and have gained recognition for their quality.

International Competitors: Cameroon's coffee competes with products from other coffee-producing countries in Africa such as Ethiopia, Kenya, and Ivory Coast. Additionally, coffee from Central and South American countries like Colombia, Brazil, and Guatemala also pose competition in the specialty coffee market due to their established reputations and large production volumes

Domestic Competitors: In Cameroon, major tea producers and exporters such as Ndawara tea, Kuku Tea Company, and Mulin Japan One are significant competitors. These companies focus on producing high-quality orthodox and CTC teas for both domestic and international markets.

International Competitors:Cameroon's tea competes with tea produced in other African countries like Kenya, Malawi, and Tanzania, which are known for their large-scale tea plantations and established tea industries. Additionally, tea-producing countries in Asia, including India, China, and Sri Lanka, are competitors in the global tea market.

Domestic Competitors: In Cameroon, horticulture producers and exporters such as Kribi Ltd, Minagri Horticulture, and CDC are key competitors. These companies focus on producing and exporting a variety of fruits, vegetables, and flowers for both regional and international markets.

International Competitors: Cameroun's horticultural products compete with imports from other Africa countries such as Kenya, South Africa and Ethiopia which have well-established horticulture industries and export network Additionally, horticultural products from countries in Europe, Asia and South America pose competition in International markets due to factors such as lower production costs and established trade relationships.
