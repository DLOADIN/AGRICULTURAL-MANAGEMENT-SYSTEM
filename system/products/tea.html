TEA

Tea farming in Cameroon is predominantly located in the western highlands of the country, particularly
in regions such as the North West and South West provinces. These areas offer suitable
climate conditions and elevation for tea cultivation.

-Duration of Farming Process:Tea farming in Cameroon typically follows a cycle thatincludes planting, maintenance (pruning, weeding, fertilizing),
and harvesting. The duration from planting to first harvest can vary depending on the tea variety but generally ranges from 2 to 3 years. After establishment, tea bushes can continue to produce for many years with proper care

-Characteristics of the Crop Cultivated in Cameroon:Cameroon cultivates various tea varieties, including Camellia sinensis var.
sinensis and Camellia sinensis var. assamica. These tea plants produce leaves that are processed to produce different types of tea, such as black,
green, oolong, and white tea.

-Climate/Temperature Needed:Tea plants thrive in subtropical to tropical climates with well-distributed rainfall and moderate temperatures.
Ideal growing conditions for tea cultivation in Cameroon include temperatures ranging from 20°C to 30°C and annual rainfall
of 1,500mm to 2,500mm. Tea plants prefer acidic, well-draining soils with good organic content.
In Cameroon, tea is cultivated in soils with pH levels ranging from 4.5 to 6.5, often found in volcanic or loamy soils.
